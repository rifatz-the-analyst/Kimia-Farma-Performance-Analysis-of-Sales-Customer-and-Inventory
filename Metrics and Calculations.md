## Metrics
The following business metrics were developed using SQL in Google BigQuery and visualized in Looker Studio to evaluate sales performance, customer retention, market penetration, and inventory optimization across the pharmacy retail network.

| **Metric**                                | **Formula / Calculation**                                                                                | **Business Purpose**                                                                                                 |
| ----------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Gross Sales**                           | Sum of selling prices before discounts                                                                   | Measures the total value of products sold before promotional discounts.                                              |
| **Net Sales**                             | Gross Sales − Discount Amount                                                                            | Measures the actual revenue generated after discounts.                                                               |
| **Gross Profit**                          | Net Sales − Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)                                                                    | Measures the profit generated from product sales.                                                                    |
| **Profit Margin (%)**                     | (Gross Profit ÷ Net Sales) × 100                                                                         | Measures the profitability of sales after accounting for product costs.                                              |
| **Sales Contribution (%)**                | (Sales of Product/Category/Province ÷ Total Net Sales) × 100                                             | Measures how much each product, category, branch, or province contributes to overall sales.                          |
| **Repeat Purchase Rate (%)**              | (Customers with more than one purchase ÷ Total Customers) × 100                                          | Measures customer loyalty by identifying the proportion of customers who made multiple purchases.                    |
| **Top Customer Revenue Contribution (%)** | (Revenue generated by the top customer segment ÷ Total Net Sales) × 100                                  | Measures the concentration of revenue among the highest-value customers.                                             |
| **Sales per Million Residents**           | (Province Net Sales ÷ Province Population) × 1,000,000                                                   | Measures market penetration by comparing sales performance relative to the size of the local population.             |
| **Average Daily Unit Sales**              | Total Units Sold ÷ Number of Selling Days                                                                | Estimates the average daily demand for each product.                                                                 |
| **Inventory Coverage (Days)**             | Current Inventory ÷ Average Daily Unit Sales                                                             | Estimates how many days the current inventory can satisfy expected customer demand.                                  |
| **Potential Gross Revenue Loss**          | Average Daily Unit Sales × Selling Price × Estimated Stockout Days                                       | Estimates the revenue at risk when products are unavailable due to stock shortages.                                  |
| **Coefficient of Variation (CV)**         | Standard Deviation ÷ Average Sales                                                                       | Measures the consistency of sales performance over time or across products. Lower values indicate more stable sales. |

## Business Assumptions

- Each record in the transaction table represents one product sold.
- The inventory table represents the latest inventory snapshot rather than historical inventory levels.
- Inventory coverage is estimated using the average daily unit sales over the most recent three months, assuming demand remains relatively stable.
- Products with inventory coverage above 30 days are classified as potentially overstocked.
- Potential gross revenue loss assumes that customer demand cannot be fulfilled while inventory is unavailable.
- Sales per million residents is used as a proxy for market penetration by combining sales data with provincial population data.
- Customer retention is measured annually based on customers who made purchases in both the previous and current year.
- Repeat customers are defined as customers who completed more than one transaction during the analysis period.
